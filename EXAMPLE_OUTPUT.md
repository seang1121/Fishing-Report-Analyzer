## Reading the Report

**Tides**: NOAA Mayport station gives you tide timing for the ICW. "RISING" means water is pushing into the creeks — great for bringing bait in to ambush spots like Sisters Creek.

**Solunar**: Major feeding windows are the 2-hour peak periods. The proximity indicator shows if you're inside a window ("NOW") or how long until the next one. When a major overlaps with a tide transition or dawn/dusk, that's your best shot.

**Conditions**: Real-time readings from two CO-OPS stations. Dames Point only reports water temp (that's all the station measures). The 6-hour pressure trend is key — "Falling" pressure is the single best predictor of a hot bite.

**Weather**: NWS gridpoint forecast for JAX area. Gives you wind/temp outlook for planning.

**Species**: HOT = peak season + conditions aligned. Scores factor in water temp, tide direction, pressure trend, solunar proximity, and moon phase.

**Spots**: All 9 ranked by current conditions. High wind drops exposed spots (Nassau Sound). Tide direction boosts matching spots. The top spots are where you should focus today.

**Bait & Rigs**: Top 3 species with specific live bait, cut bait, and artificial options plus the rig setup. Grab what you need at the bait shop on the way.

**Be On The Water By**: A single arrival time recommendation. The analyzer finds where dawn/dusk, solunar windows, and tide transitions overlap — that's your best shot.

**Bite Probability**: Hour-by-hour timeline for the day. FIRE/HOT hours are when multiple factors stack up. Slow hours are when you should be rigging, eating, or running to the next spot.

**Go/No-Go**: 75+ means conditions are stacked in your favor. Below 35 means stay home or hit a protected creek.
